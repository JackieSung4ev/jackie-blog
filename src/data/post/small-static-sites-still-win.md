A personal site does not need much machinery. It needs a clear home page, readable writing, durable URLs, RSS, and a way
to change direction without fighting the stack.

That is why static sites remain a good default.

## The good parts

- They are fast by default.
- They are easy to version.
- They can be hosted almost anywhere.
- They encourage smaller pages and fewer hidden dependencies.

The tradeoff is that dynamic features require extra services. For this site, that is fine. Comments, forms, search, and
analytics can be added only when they are genuinely needed.

Start with the quiet version. Let the content prove what the site needs next.
